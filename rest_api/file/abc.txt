Recurring
The King's family
Tom Glynn-Carney as Prince Aegon Targaryen: The firstborn son of King Viserys and Queen Alicent Hightower, later crowned as King Aegon II by Queen Alicent's supporters.[10] He is a dragonrider who rides the dragon Sunfyre.
Ty Tennant portrays young Aegon Targaryen.[11]
Phia Saban as Princess Helaena Targaryen: The second-born child and daughter of King Viserys and Queen Alicent Hightower, sister to Aegon and Aemond, half sister to Princess Rhaenyra.[10] She is a dragonrider who rides the she-dragon Dreamfyre.
Evie Allen portrays young Helaena Targaryen.[10]
Ewan Mitchell as Prince Aemond Targaryen: The second son of King Viserys and Queen Alicent Hightower.[10] He later rides the she-dragon Vhagar after Laena Velaryon's passing.
Leo Ashton portrays young Aemond Targaryen.[10]
Harry Collett as Prince Jacaerys Velaryon: Princess Rhaenyra's first-born son.[10] He is a dragonrider who rides the dragon Vermax.
Leo Hart portrays young Jacaerys Velaryon.[10]
Elliot Grihault as Prince Lucerys Velaryon: Princess Rhaenyra's second-born son.[10] He is a dragonrider who rides the dragon Arrax.
Harvey Sadler portrays young Lucerys Velaryon.[10]
John Macmillan as Ser Laenor Velaryon: Son of Princess Rhaenys and Lord Corlys Velaryon, and the first husband of Princess Rhaenyra. He is a dragonrider who rides the dragon Seasmoke.[12]
Theo Nate portrays young Laenor Velaryon.[12]
Matthew Carver portrays child Laenor Velaryon.
Nanna Blondell as Lady Laena Velaryon: Daughter of Corlys Velaryon and Rhaenys Targaryen, sister of Laenor Velaryon, and later the second wife of Prince Daemon Targaryen.[12][13] She is a dragonrider who rides the old, legendary she-dragon Vhagar.
Savannah Steyn portrays young Laena Velaryon.[10]
Nova Foueillis-Mose portrays child Laena Velaryon.[10]
Bethany Antonia as Lady Baela Targaryen: The eldest daughter of Prince Daemon Targaryen and Lady Laena Velaryon. She is a dragonrider who rides the young dragon Moondancer.[10]
Phoebe Campbell as Lady Rhaena Targaryen: The younger daughter of Prince Daemon Targaryen and Lady Laena Velaryon.[10]
The King's council
Gavin Spokes as Lord Lyonel Strong: Lord of Harrenhal and Master of Laws to King Viserys.[4]
David Horovitch as Grand Maester Mellos.[4]
Bill Paterson as Lord Lyman Beesbury: Lord of Honeyholt and Master of Coin on King Viserys' small council.[4]
Elliott Tittensor as Ser Erryk Cargyll: Member of Viserys' Kingsguard, as well as the twin of Ser Arryk Cargyll.[10]
Luke Tittensor as Ser Arryk Cargyll: Member of Viserys' Kingsguard, as well as the twin of Ser Erryk Cargyll.[10]
Anthony Flanagan as Ser Steffon Darklyn, a member of the Kingsguard.[10]
Phil Daniels as Maester Gerardys: One of the Maesters of the Citadel.[10]
Kurt Egyiawan as Maester Orwyle: One of the Maesters of the Citadel.[10]
Paul Kennedy as Jasper Wylde: known as "Ironrod", the Lord of Rain House and Head of House Wylde.[10]
Other lords and knights of the Realm
Steffan Rhodri as Hobert Hightower: The head of House Hightower and ruler of Oldtown. He is the older brother of Ser Otto Hightower who wanted Otto's grandson Aegon to be the heir apparent.[10]
Julian Lewis Jones as Boremund Baratheon: the Lord of Storm's End and head of House Baratheon early in the reign of King Viserys I Targaryen. He is also the maternal uncle of Rhaenys Targaryen.[10]
Roger Evans as Ser Borros Baratheon: Son of Lord Boremund Baratheon.[10]
Ryan Corr as Ser Harwin Strong: The eldest son to Lord Lyonel Strong and heir to Harrenhal. Known as "Harwin Breakbones", he is said to be the strongest man in the Seven Kingdom.[4]
Wil Johnson as Ser Vaemond Velaryon: Younger brother to Lord Corlys Velaryon, and a commander in the Velaryon navy.[12]
Solly McLeod as Ser Joffrey Lonmouth: a knight of House Lonmouth, the battle companion and the lover to Laenor Velaryon.[10]
Guest
Sian Brooke as Queen Aemma Arryn: the queen consort and first wife of King Viserys Targaryen.[10] She is a granddaughter of King Jaehaerys Targaryen through her mother, Princess Daella Targaryen, making Viserys her first cousin. She died giving birth to Prince Baelon, who also died within the day of his birth.[5]
Michael Carter as King Jaehaerys I Targaryen: The fourth king of the Seven Kingdoms, and the grandfather of King Viserys, Prince Daemon Targaryen, Princess Rhaenys Targaryen and Queen Aemma Arryn. Known as the "Conciliator" or the "Old King", he outlived all his children, and eventually had to organize a great council to choose heir from his grandchildren.
Daniel Scott-Smith as Craghas "Crabfeeder" Drahar: A Myrish prince admiral who served the Triarchy and conquered the Stepstones, plaguing the Westerosi sea trade.
Rachel Redford as Lady Rhea Royce: The Lady of Runestone and Daemon Targaryen's estranged first wife.
